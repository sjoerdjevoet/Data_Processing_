Definition of indexes

Quality of Life Index (QLI) is concerned with the general well-being of individuals and societies, outlining negative and positive features of life. It observes life satisfaction, including everything from physical health, family, education, employment, wealth, religious beliefs, finance and the environment.

Safety Index is the state of being "safe"; the condition of being protected from harm or other non-desirable outcomes.

Health Care Index is the degree to which the quality of the countries, as mentally and physically.

The cost of living index indicates how expensive products and services are compared to other countries.

Following the data
Within this map, the Quality of Life Indexes (QLI) are compared among countries. Furthermore, the variables Safety Index, Health Care Index and Cost of living Index are also compared among the countries in order to zoom in on the term "Quality of Life Index". Looking at the data in 2016 it has become clear that the continent NORTH AMERICA has a pretty high Quality of Life Index. On the contray, the continent ASIA, seems to have a pretty low QLI; compared to the other continents. In 2017, Canada has a substantial loss in QLI. On the other hand, the QLI of Ireland seems to have grown. There also seems to be a relationship between a relative high QLI and the corresponding components as mentioned (Cost of living index, etc).
